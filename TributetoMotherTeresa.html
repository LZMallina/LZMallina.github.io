Mother Teresa

Mother to the unwanted
(August 26, 1910-September 5, 1997)

The Life of Mother Teresa Outlined:

  • 1910: Born as Mary Teresa Bojaxhius in Albania
  • 1928: Committed herself to a religious life and joined the sisters of Loreto at Loreto Abby in Rathfamham, Ireland.
  • 1929: Arrived in India and begin teaching at St. Teresa's school.
  • 1931: Teresa took her first religious vows
  • 1944: Appointed as the headmistress of Loreto convent school in Entally, Eastern Calcutta.
  • 1943 and 1946: Teresa was extremely disturbed by the misery and death around her brought on by the Bengal famine and Direct Action Day (A period of Muslium-Hindu violence>
  • 1946: Mother Teresa received her calling from God to begin her missionary work with the poorest of the poor.
  • 1949: With no income, Teresa, joined by a group of young women, go dooor to door begged for food and supplies to help the poor.
  • 1950: Received permission and support from the Vatican to establish Missionaries of Charity, which comprise of 13 members. Missionaries of Charity provided care to the unwanted: the hungry, the naked, the homeless, the crippled, the blind, the lepers, and all those shunned by society.
  • 1952: Teresa opened her first hospice Nirmal Hirday (Home of the Pure Heart) free for the poor to die with dignity
  • 1955: She opened Shanti Nagar (City of peace) for those with leprosy, and Nirmala Shishu Bhavan (The Children's Home of the Immaculate Heart) for homeless children and orphans.
  • 1960-2007: Missionaries of Charity expanded throughout the world, including the United States, to provided care to the poor.
  • 1979: Mother Teresa received the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1997: Mother Teresa died in Calcultta. At the time of her death, the Missionaries of Charity operates more than 517 missions in over 100 countries. Her missions operate hospices and homes for people with HIV/AIDS, leprosy and tuberculosis, soup kitchens, children's and family counseling programs, orphages, and schools.
